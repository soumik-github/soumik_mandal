# 3d vision, speech, and language - multimodal

## What is 3D Vision?

**3D Vision** refers to computer systems that can perceive and understand three-dimensional spatial information from the real world. Unlike regular 2D images, 3D vision captures depth, allowing systems to:
- Reconstruct 3D scenes and objects
- Understand spatial relationships and distances
- Perform tasks like autonomous navigation, object manipulation, and scene understanding

**Major Technologies:**
- **Stereo Vision**: Using multiple cameras to estimate depth
- **LiDAR**: Laser-based depth sensing (used in autonomous vehicles)
- **RGB-D Cameras**: Combine color (RGB) with depth data
- **Structure from Motion**: Reconstructing 3D shapes from multiple 2D views
- **Neural 3D Reconstruction**: Using deep learning (NeRF, 3D GANs)

## What is Speech Multimodal?

**Speech Multimodal** systems combine speech with other modalities (vision, text, etc.) to improve understanding. This overcomes limitations of speech-only systems:
- **Visual context matters**: Lip movements help recognize speech in noisy environments
- **Emotions and intent**: Facial expressions and tone provide crucial context
- **Robustness**: Multiple input sources make the system more reliable

## 3D Vision + Speech Multimodal

When combined, these create powerful systems:
- **3D Gesture Recognition**: Understanding hand/body movements in 3D space during conversation
- **Immersive AI Assistants**: Systems that understand your 3D environment, movements, and voice together
- **Robotics & Embodied AI**: Robots perceiving 3D environments while understanding spoken commands with context
- **AR/VR Applications**: Spatial understanding with voice interaction

**Challenges:**
- High computational cost
- Data synchronization across modalities
- Real-time processing requirements
